Applications of geospatial technologies

  • Spatialisation of outbreaks (space-time patterns and causes)
  • Hosts and vector distribution mapping
  • Estimation of environmental indices and monitoring of favorable environmental conditions
  • Disease risk mapping and prediction of number of cases
  • Landscape analysis: metrics, fragmentation, LULCC detection
  • Optimal distribution of health services
  • Location of best routes to hospitals
  • etc.

Gaps, opportunities and challenges

Difficulties and challenges I

  • Compromise between different resolutions
  • VHR, LiDAR, UAV, Hyper-spectral (U$S)
  • Missing data, clouds, shadows (spatial and temporal interpolations)
  • Need for corrections of various types
  • Large volumes of data vs. bandwidth, storage and computational capacity (cloud computing, parallelisation, learning time and U$S)

Field data will always be needed! :)

Difficulties and challenges II

  • Updating and digitisation of disease cases and intervention data
  • Harmonisation of records at different levels and from different systems
  • Facilitating data access and public access to aggregated health data
  • Training of staff
  • Development of standardised, reproducible and scalable methods
  • Commitment and allocation of resources
